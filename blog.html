Garden lizard

It was a bright sunny day and it must have been over 38Â°C. Around 3 in the afternoon, when I charged my phone, cleaned my glasses and headed towards the front yard expecting nothing.
I was waiting for a shot and trying to be as quiet as possible. And as the background music, bird's song was there. Right then, I heard a rustle in the bushes which was near the tomato plants. I peered in the cluster and there, camouflaged among the leaves, I saw it.
Slowly, I approached a minimum distance to get a sharp shot of it. It was very aware of my presence but allowed me to photograph it. But this was not for long when I tried for another angle which scared him and he fled.
The 'Garden lizard', as its name suggests, is the most common agamid of India. Photographing it with mobile phone wasn't an easy task I must say, but I did good I guess.

Photography

Lesser toad

It was showering and I was photographing some butterflies in the garden, that's when I saw this little frog hopping around. Slowly I approached, but I, being a giant in his eyes, he was scared of me. It took me some time to gain his trust and finally he allowed me to photograph him. This is my first time photographing a tiny baby frog. Man those eyes were so hypnotic, its beauty can not be justified in a photograph.
I love witnessing these speechless beauty of nature which motivates me to get out and photograph every day.
